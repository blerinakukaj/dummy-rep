---


**Say this:**

## SCENARIO 2 — Metric Drop (SearchBoost Query Engine)

**Duration:** ~1.5 minutes
**[SPEAKER: Intern 2 — owns Metrics Agent]**

### What to type

```powershell

```

---

### STEP 1 — Introduce (15 seconds)

**Say this:**

> "SearchBoost Query Engine. The problem: search relevance dropped 15% after deploying version 2.4.1. Seven tickets — relevance drop, user complaints, increased mobile latency, missing analytics events. Relevance went from 0.85 to 0.72, zero result rate is 18%."

---

### STEP 2 — Show the experiment plan (60 seconds)

**Type:**
```powershell
code "$base/artifacts/experiment_plan.md"
```

**Say this:**

> "Here's the experiment plan. Clear hypothesis: 'If we improve the algorithm, then relevance increases by 5.88 percent from 0.85 to 0.9.' Success metrics defined with baselines and targets. Guardrail metric: Session Abandonment — currently 0.28, max acceptable 0.25. If it degrades, we stop."
>
> "Four-phase rollout: 10%, 25%, 50%, 100% — each 7 days. If relevance doesn't hit 0.87 after phase one, halt and reassess. Sample size 1000 users per phase — that comes from the policy file. Rollback criteria explicit. Analysis plan uses paired t-test with Holm-Bonferroni correction."

---

### STEP 3 — Mention instrumentation gaps (15 seconds)

**Say this:**

> "The Metrics Agent also flagged instrumentation gaps — missing events like image_search_query, voice_search_query, filter_applied. So it didn't just build an experiment plan — it told you what you're missing to even measure properly."

## SCENARIO 4 — Policy Configurability (Same Input, Different Outcome)

**Duration:** ~2.5 minutes ⭐ KEY MOMENT
**[SPEAKER: Intern 3 — owns Risk Agent + policies]**

---

### STEP 1 — Show the two policy files (30 seconds)

**Say this:**

> "Same privacy_risk bundle from Scenario 1 — PersonaLens User Profiling — but run through two different policies: default and startup fast."

**Type:**
```powershell
Write-Host "=== DEFAULT POLICY ==="; Select-String "max_unmitigated|require_legal_review" src/aipm/policies/default_policy.yaml; Write-Host ""; Write-Host "=== STARTUP POLICY ==="; Select-String "max_unmitigated|require_legal_review" src/aipm/policies/startup_fast_policy.yaml
```

**Say this:**

> "Two key differences: Default has max_unmitigated_high_risks of 2 and require_legal_review_for_pii true. Startup has max of 5 and legal review false. Two numbers changed."

---

### STEP 2 — Compare recommendations (30 seconds)

**Type:**
```powershell
$a = (Get-Content "output/demo_11a_default_policy/8a1afe3b-82b0-44e4-b2b8-76abd4a9f3f9/artifacts/final_plan.json" -Raw | ConvertFrom-Json).recommendation; $b = (Get-Content "output/demo_11b_startup_policy/a8348979-0ada-4952-a88f-9145d66087c4/artifacts/final_plan.json" -Raw | ConvertFrom-Json).recommendation; Write-Host "DEFAULT POLICY  -> $($a.decision)"; Write-Host "STARTUP POLICY  -> $($b.decision)"
```

**Say this:**

> "Default policy: do not pursue. Startup policy: validate first. Same product idea. Same tickets. Same data. The only difference is the policy YAML."

---

### STEP 3 — Explain WHY (90 seconds)

**Type:**
```powershell
Write-Host "=== DEFAULT RISK GATE ==="; Get-Content "output/demo_11a_default_policy/8a1afe3b-82b0-44e4-b2b8-76abd4a9f3f9/findings/risk_gate_result.json"; Write-Host ""; Write-Host "=== STARTUP RISK GATE ==="; Get-Content "output/demo_11b_startup_policy/a8348979-0ada-4952-a88f-9145d66087c4/findings/risk_gate_result.json"
```

**Say this:**

> "Look at the risk gates side by side. Default policy has 3 blockers: Inadequate Consent, GDPR Audit Required, and — this one — '3 unmitigated high risks exceed maximum of 2.' That's the policy rule. Plus 2 warnings about legal review required."
>
> "Startup policy: only 2 blockers — the same consent and GDPR ones, because critical privacy risks still block even in startup mode. But the third blocker disappeared — we had 3 high risks, but max is now 5, so under the limit. And zero warnings because require_legal_review_for_pii is false."
>
> "The startup policy says: critical privacy risks still block you — we're not reckless — but we're more tolerant of high-severity risks and we don't require legal review at this stage. That's enough to move the recommendation from 'kill it' to 'validate and proceed.'"
>
> "This is the power of the policy engine. Product teams can tune risk tolerance without changing any code."
