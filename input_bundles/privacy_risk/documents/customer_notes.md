# Customer Interview Notes — PersonaLens User Profiling

## Interview 1: Sarah M. (Power User, Daily Active)
**Date:** 2026-01-08

Sarah loves personalized content feeds. "The more relevant stuff I see, the longer I stay. I discovered three new creators last month because the algorithm just *gets* me." When asked about data collection, she paused: "I guess I assumed you only tracked what I clicked on, not how long I hovered or scrolled. That feels... different." She said she'd want to know exactly what's being tracked and would appreciate a clear dashboard. "If I found out you were sharing my data with advertisers without telling me, I'd delete my account immediately."

## Interview 2: Marcus T. (Enterprise Admin, IT Manager)
**Date:** 2026-01-10

Marcus oversees 200+ employee accounts. "Personalization for our team's content library would be great — people waste time searching for relevant docs." However, he raised concerns about cross-device tracking: "Our employees use personal phones for work. Tracking their browsing on personal devices is a non-starter for us. We'd need device-level opt-outs, not just user-level." He also flagged GDPR: "We have EU-based employees. If your profiling system can't demonstrate lawful basis under Article 6, our legal team will block the integration."

## Interview 3: Priya K. (Casual User, Weekly Active)
**Date:** 2026-01-14

Priya is lukewarm on personalization. "Sometimes I like discovering random things, not just stuff the algorithm thinks I want. It can feel like a filter bubble." On privacy: "I took a survey last month and said I was concerned about privacy. I don't think anything changed. I still get targeted ads that feel creepy — like it knows I was looking at flights to Lisbon." She mentioned she tried to find privacy settings but gave up: "There were too many toggles and none of them clearly explained what they did."

## Survey Summary (n=1,200 respondents, January 2026)
- **72%** of users say personalized recommendations improve their experience
- **40%** expressed moderate-to-high concern about how their behavioral data is used
- **28%** said they would reduce usage if they learned browsing history was tracked in detail
- **15%** have attempted to find and modify privacy settings (only 6% succeeded)
- **52%** said they would value a transparency dashboard showing collected data
- **61%** said they would not consent to sharing data with advertising partners
