# Customer Interview Notes — QuickPay Checkout

## Interview 1: Sarah M. (Power User, 3+ orders/week)

> "I love how fast Amazon's checkout is — one click and done. With QuickPay, I have to re-enter my card every time because the 'save payment' option doesn't seem to work on my phone. It's 2026, this should be instant."

> "Last week my checkout crashed twice on my iPhone. I had a full cart both times and had to start over. Almost switched to CompetitorX because at least their app doesn't crash."

> "Speed is important but honestly, I'd rather wait 5 extra seconds than get double-charged. That happened to my friend and she still hasn't gotten her refund."

## Interview 2: James R. (Occasional Buyer, 2-3 orders/month)

> "I don't trust auto-applying coupons — I always worry it's picking the wrong one or there's a catch. I'd rather see all my options and pick myself. But I do hate having to hunt for promo codes."

> "The checkout feels slow compared to other sites. I timed it once — took me 45 seconds from cart to confirmation. That's way too long when competitors do it in under 20 seconds."

> "Reliability matters more than bells and whistles. I had a payment fail last month and the money was taken from my account but the order didn't go through. Took 5 days to get it sorted. That's unacceptable."

## Interview 3: Maria L. (Enterprise Buyer, Procurement Team)

> "For our team purchases, we need the checkout to be rock-solid. We process 50+ orders a month and can't afford payment failures — it creates accounting nightmares."

> "Social proof badges would actually be annoying for B2B purchases. We don't care that '23 people bought this' — we care that the payment goes through reliably and we get proper invoices."

> "If you're going to add features, add better payment method management. Let us save multiple cards and set a default. But fix the crashes first."

## Summary of Themes

- **Speed**: Users want faster checkout (one-click, saved payments)
- **Reliability**: Payment failures and crashes are top complaints and erode trust
- **Trust**: Double-charging incidents have created fear; reliability > speed for many users
- **B2B needs**: Enterprise buyers prioritize reliability and proper tooling over consumer features
