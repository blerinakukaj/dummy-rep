# Customer Notes — EnterpriseSuite

## Interview 1: David L. (CTO, Fortune-500 Prospect)
David's team evaluated EnterpriseSuite for 6 months but procurement is blocked on SSO and data isolation. "We can't deploy a SaaS tool that doesn't support SAML — it's a non-starter for our security team." He estimated their organization would bring 2,000 seats at the enterprise tier if these requirements are met. He also raised concerns about vendor lock-in, asking for data export APIs and contractual SLAs around uptime (99.95% minimum).

## Interview 2: Rachel M. (VP Engineering, Mid-Market Customer)
Rachel's 150-person team is an existing customer but has hit scaling walls. "The permissions model is too simple — I have to give everyone admin just to let them manage their own projects." She described spending 3 hours per week manually adjusting access. Her security team also flagged that audit logs don't capture permission changes, which is a SOC 2 audit finding.

## Interview 3: Kenji S. (Head of Compliance, APAC Enterprise Prospect)
Kenji requires data residency in the Tokyo region for regulatory compliance. "Japanese financial services regulations require all customer data to remain within Japan. Cross-region replication would disqualify your platform." He also needs detailed data processing agreements and GDPR-equivalent privacy controls. His team would license 500 seats pending compliance verification.
