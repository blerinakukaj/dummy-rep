# Customer Notes — LearnPath Accessibility Gaps

## Interview 1: Dr. Susan M. (Head of Digital Learning, State University)
Dr. Susan's university is evaluating LMS platforms for 30,000 students. "We are legally required to meet Section 508 and WCAG 2.1 AA for all educational technology. LearnPath's feature set is exactly what we need, but our accessibility review found 47 WCAG violations. We cannot proceed until these are resolved." She emphasized that 12% of their student body has registered disabilities, and any inaccessible tool exposes the university to lawsuit risk under the ADA.

## Interview 2: James R. (Visually Impaired User, Corporate Training)
James uses JAWS screen reader and is enrolled in mandatory corporate training through LearnPath at his employer. "I literally cannot complete my required training courses. The course catalog reads as a flat wall of text with no structure, the video player is completely unusable with a keyboard, and the quizzes are impossible — I can't tell which radio button I'm selecting." He has filed a formal ADA complaint with his employer's HR department.

## Interview 3: Emily K. (Procurement Manager, Government Agency)
Emily's agency needs a VPAT before any purchasing decision. "No VPAT means no contract — it's a hard procurement requirement under Section 508. We've seen your product demo and it's strong functionally, but our accessibility office needs to review a conformance statement. Our contract would be $400K annually covering 5,000 employees across multiple departments."
